About Me

Hey, I’m Jefferson — a Mathematics–Computer Science student at UC San Diego, and a first-generation college student navigating tech with heart, purpose, and curiosity.

Growing up, I didn’t have access to tutors or mentors in tech — I taught myself how to code the same way I learned guitar: slowly, persistently, and with a lot of trial and error. I’ve been playing guitar for over 10 years, and the way it trains patience, improvisation, and rhythm has shaped how I think as a developer. Whether it’s music or algorithms, I find joy in the flow of solving problems and expressing ideas.

I’m especially drawn to projects that serve real people. At UCSD’s DiamondHacks, I helped build ShelterGuide, a web app to help unhoused individuals find emergency shelters. It was just a prototype — but building it showed me how tech can meet real needs when we take the time to listen. As a mentor in Project ECHO, I guided LAUSD students through the process of turning community issues into business ideas — helping our team earn 2nd place out of dozens of schools with a pitch that tackled homelessness through youth-driven innovation.

I’m also passionate about making AI more transparent and responsible. I explored these questions in my Naive Bayes lie detector project, and later in a weather predictor using Hidden Markov Models. Now, I’m part of a faculty-led research group on interpretable machine learning under Professor Sanjoy Dasgupta. It’s been powerful to explore the ethics and structure of AI — not just the math, but the meaning.

Beyond code, I care deeply about the planet. At the Santa Rosa Island Research Station, I worked hands-on with environmental restoration teams to protect fragile ecosystems and understand the role we play in preserving biodiversity. It grounded me — and reminded me that the systems we build, digital or ecological, depend on our willingness to care for them.

Today, I’m someone who loves solving LeetCode problems (top 2% worldwide), mentoring others, building tools that matter, and finding beauty in both structure and chaos. I’m constantly learning, constantly building — but always rooted in where I came from and the communities I want to serve. My dream is to use software, research, and education to make tech more ethical, more accessible, and more human.

Weather Prediction with Hidden Markov Models

→ Python | CSE 150A Project

  • Developed a probabilistic time-series classifier using a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) to forecast next-day rain/snow.
  • Engineered HMM parameters including transition matrix, emission matrix, and initial state distribution from 2,500+ labeled climate records.
  • Applied the Forward Algorithm to enable real-time prediction through a command-line interface.
  • Achieved 53% validation accuracy; identified limitations due to seasonal drift and binary discretization.
